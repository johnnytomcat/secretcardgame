How to Play

Overview

A social deduction game set in 1930s Germany. Liberals work together to stop the Fascists from seizing power, while Fascists secretly conspire to elect their leader.

Teams & Roles

Role Distribution (4-6 players):

  • 4 players: 2 Liberals, 1 Fascist, 1 Hitler
  • 5 players: 3 Liberals, 1 Fascist, 1 Hitler
  • 6 players: 4 Liberals, 1 Fascist, 1 Hitler

Team Knowledge:

  • Liberals: Don't know anyone's role
  • Fascists: Know who Hitler is
  • Hitler: Doesn't know who the Fascists are

How to Win

Liberals Win If:

  • 5 Liberal policies are enacted, OR
  • Hitler is executed

Fascists Win If:

  • 6 Fascist policies are enacted, OR
  • Hitler is elected Chancellor after 3+ Fascist policies are enacted

Game Flow

  1. Election: The President nominates a Chancellor candidate.
  2. Voting: All players vote JA (Yes) or NEIN (No). Majority wins.
  3. Legislative Session: If the vote passes:
    • President draws 3 policies, passes 2 to Chancellor
    • Chancellor enacts 1 policy
  4. Executive Action: The President may gain special powers (see below).
  5. Next Round: Presidency passes to the next player.

Executive Powers

When Fascist policies are enacted, the President gains powers:

  • 3rd Fascist Policy: Examine top 3 cards in the deck
  • 4th Fascist Policy: Execute a player
  • 5th Fascist Policy: Execute a player

Execution Rules:

  • The President must choose a player to execute
  • Executed players are eliminated and cannot vote or hold office
  • If Hitler is executed, Liberals win immediately!
  • The executed player's role is revealed to all

Special Rules

  • Term Limits: The previous President and Chancellor cannot be nominated as Chancellor
  • Hitler Danger Zone: After 3 Fascist policies, electing Hitler as Chancellor wins the game for Fascists
  • Election Tracker: If 3 governments fail in a row, the top policy is automatically enacted (chaos!)
  • Deck Reshuffle: When fewer than 3 cards remain, the deck is reshuffled

Strategy Tips

  • Liberals: Pay attention to voting patterns and policy claims. Trust no one completely!
  • Fascists: Blend in with Liberals. Create confusion and distrust.
  • Hitler: Act like a Liberal. Your team wins if you become Chancellor late game.
